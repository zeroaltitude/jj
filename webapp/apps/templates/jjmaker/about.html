About JotJournal

JotJournal was founded on the idea that many people want to have keepsakes about their lives but are way too lazy to make scrapbooks and photo albums or keep a diary.  JotJournal makes the process of keeping your moments easy and fun.  Just click a button and we'll turn your Facebook status updates and photos into a real, printed book you can buy and keep.  You get an impressive, adorable keepsake without having to do any actual work!  And you can feel virtuous about all of the time you spend on Facebook to boot.  Sweet!

The people behind JotJournal

Liesel Pollvogt, Founder

JotJournal is the brainchild of Liesel Pollvogt, a mom of two who worked at the photo-sharing start-up Tabblo and later in HP's printing division where she helped create fun photo apps for Facebook and MySpace.  You can read more about Liesel and the inspiration behind JotJournal here.  Liesel likes to post on Facebook and feels guilty that she never finished her now 7-year-old daughter's baby book.  She lives south of Boston with her husband, son, daughter and two very old cats who might in fact be zombies.

Eddie Abrams, Code Maestro

 

Eddie takes Liesel's crazy ideas and makes them real with code so beautiful it sings.  Liesel and Eddie worked together at Tabblo and HP and yet somehow he still agreed to help her with this new venture.  When he's not coding, Eddie's kicking it at Drum 'n Bass night or hanging out in Worcester with his family but certainly not sleeping.

Brian Cook, The Designing Farmer

Brian creates sophisticated designs for JotJournal and other much less interesting companies like Google and Goodby while raising chickens and peacocks on a farm in upstate New York with his wife and two daughters.  If you're ever on the phone with Brian and you hear what sounds like a baby screeching hysterically it is just the peacock looking for his mate who for some reason is hiding.  Oh, and Brian also turned an old chicken coop into a tiki bar.  Which adds nothing to this bio but is pretty awesome.
